Jersey European Airways, the precursor to Flybe

Launched in 1979 Jersey European Airways was sold in 1983 to the Walker Steel Group owned by Jack Walker

1979 - 1983

Cancer strikes

In 2000 Jack Walker dies from cancer. Jersey European Airways goes on to become British European and then in 2002 becomes Flybe.

2000 Aug 17

Flybe IPO

At the time of the IPO shares were issued at 295p valuing the company at £215m. Several investors took on large stakes. Perhaps most notable was the biomedical research charity, The Wellcome Trust who had bought a 4.96% stake by 12 Nov 2013 estimated to be worth over £5m. By 2016 they had grown this to 6.2%

Fuel and USD hedged

Mitigating the risks identified in the H1 2018/19 Trading update at this point we know from the half year results to be published in Nov 14 that the company has paid for:

  • 90.0% of its anticipated fuel requirements for the next 12 months
  • 52.6% of its anticipated USD requirements for the next 12 months

Thats is £50m worth of fuel bought just after the most recent peak. To put prices into perspective here is 5 years of fuel prices.

As fuel is purchased in USD we must also consider the currency rate
